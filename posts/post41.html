Motivation Study

Comparing extrinsic motivations

October 21st, 2017

Study Criteria


Motivation study A:

Motivation study B:

Participant asked to drink 4 glasses of water and report that consumption back to researcher via SMS for the following consecutive 4 days. If they are adherent, I researcher sends $10 to participant.

Motivation study C:

Require participant to select a reward they will give themselves upon successful completion of the study. Participant asked to drink 4 glasses of water and report that consumption back to researcher via SMS for the following consecutive 4 days. If they are adherent they give themselves the reward they outlined at the outset of the study.

Results

Conclusions

Study A, which required users to earn their own money back was the most effective, perhaps because fear of lose is greater than drive to earn rewards. Study C was reasonably effective but perhaps failed because users determined that they could still get themselves the reward they chose regardless of their behavior within the user test.
