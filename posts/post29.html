Desk Buddy

Feed your feelings.

February 25th, 2017

Design Question

How might we use peopleâ€™s relationships with objects to provide a release from anxiety?

The domains for this project were ritualistic objects, dependency, power, control, and the extension of self.

Design Values

  • Speculative
  • Personifiying objects
  • Manipulates relationship between people and objects.

Precedents

My precedents for the color changing based off emotion aspect were simple mood rings. While mood rings determine emotion based off skin temeprature, Desk Buddy would read the note fed to him and determine emotion based off of the message.

First Prototype

I started with creating a basic look and feel prototype. I cut out a rectangle in order to create a mouth, and drew a friendly face on the box. I asked my classmates to write down their anxieties and feed it to their desk pet.

The general feedback was that the desk pet was endearing, but users wanted some way of actually destroying the paper their emotions were written on. Additionally, they wanted to be able to feed the desk pet happy emotions as well.

Second Prototype

Using this feedback I revamped my concept. Instead of just destroying anxieties, desk buddy would consume all softs of feelings and glow different colors based of the emotions his diet consisted of; red for anger, blue for calm, yellow for nervous, and green for happy.

My first step was creating the foam shell for the Desk Buddy to fit inside of. I cut pieces of black foam board to create sides and a bottom to contain the parts.

I added googly eyes to help personify the box, and make him seem friendlier.

Next I added an RGB strip connected by bluetooth to an application which controlls the color.

The colors of the Desk Buddy switch according to what emotions it is fed. Red symbolizes that the user is anxious, blue indicates calm, yellow stand for nervous, and green represents happy.

As a third iteration I added ears to the deskBuddy, to give him more animalistic qualities.
