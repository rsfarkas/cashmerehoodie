Final Project Post Mortem

Soft Skills For Children.

December 9th, 2016

Post Mortem

The hypothesized results of the project are that children will engage with the monkey and will be motivated to play the game. Having a physical object with which to relate will help children use imaginative play to learn soft skills.

Due to the limited nature of the physical prototype, it was challenging to gain a real understanding of the value of the vibration feedback. The interface of the game was friendly and engaging, and users enjoyed being able to press the light on the arm of the physical prototype. The interaction between the physical and digital prototypes turned out to be not as important as originally expected, but the value of having a physical prototype with which the user could relate was priceless.

The more interactive the storybook, the more the users were engaged. Clicking through screens was interpreted like turning pages. Users felt more in control of the decision-making process and their learning speed. Were this project to be taken further, it would be best to create an entire scenario connected to the physical prototype using Processing and Firmata to better user-test the vibration.
