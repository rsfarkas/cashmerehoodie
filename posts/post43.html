      Process and User Testing

      There were three key changes made in this prototype. The first was the look and feel. In previous iterations younger users felt like the application was too serious and medical. In order to lighten up the mood the color pallete shifted from muted greens and blues to include more vibrant colors, such as purple and yellow. Thicker borders were added to the PHI cards to make them feel more like tiles, and a heavier use of iconography was adopted.


      The second major change was that self-checks were broken into more manageable step-by-step processes. The goal of this change was to make self-checks feel less scary and less time consuming.

      The third changes was the information hierarchy of the dashboard.The focus of the application is supposed to be the PHI so moving this directly beneath the progress bar made that more clear. Respondents agreed the notes were the least important and felt that an overall activity chart was completely unnecessary.

      Reminder Component

      One of the key elements I wanted to address and test in this prototype was function of in home reminders. I built a sample LCD screen that I hung over testers medecine cabinets. The location was chosen because users typically store medicine and other hygene products here, and were likely to be thinking about their apperance and health.

      Feedback Response

      After presenting my functional prototype I recieved a lot of valuable feedback. The reminder LCD screen is extremely well recieved and I believe it will be important to figure out ways to make the connection with the application stronger through the message displayed on the screen and the physical presentation of the device. I still want to investigate other integrations with devices like the amazon dot, and user test to determine which is most effective at reminding users to use the application.

      The look and feel of the application is on the right track but testing out patterns which introduce new shapes besides for the main icon used might add a level of interest to each screen. Additionally, the step by step process for self-checks makes users feel more comfortable and less overwhelmed. Next steps include determining which relevant conditions have possible self-checks, and which conditions have several self-checks that need to be done in tandem for effective monitoring and results.

      There is also much work to be done on the extrinsic motivation reward system. Possible iterations will include differnet actions having different associated monitary values, as well as social sharing, leaderboards, and levels.
