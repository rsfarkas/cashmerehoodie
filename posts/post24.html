Final Project Digital Prototype

Soft Skills For Children.

December 8th, 2016

Final Digital Prototype

In response to the user testing, I decided that in order for the digital prototype to meet the design values, it needed to allow the users to learn at their own pace. It was important that playing with Benny was fun, and that they did not feel the pressure of a timer.

After listening to all of my user testing recordings, it seemed like users responded best to clicking action. It is something that is inherent to iPhone and iPad games, and made the users feel like were in control, one of the design values intended feelings.The clicking motion simulates the turning of pages, which is valuable to making the digital experience feel more like an interactive story book.

I also made the decision to remove health points from the interactive storybook. It is not a game, and the points made it feel to much like one could win or lose, going against the design values. Based off of the user tests I believe that children will be motivated to play with the interactive story book because its enjoyable and because the physical prototype is friendly engaging, allowing them to connect when pressing the light on the arm.The interaction between the physical and digital prototypes turned out to be not as important as originally expected, but the value of having a physical prototype with which the user could relate was priceless.
